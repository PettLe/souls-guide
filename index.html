Praise the Sun!

From Software's Soulsborne series has become a synonym with crueling difficulty. While these games are (most of the time) fair and their reputation has been HUGELY exaggerated, no doubt they still are hard as nails. Especially in the beginning! These games don't hold your hand or tell you about the crucial mechanics which would make your journey that much easier. So here is a small guide for each of the games, telling you the very basic tips and tricks for a smoother start! This guide isn't for people who want a step-by-step guide through the early game but for those who wish to actually figure out the game on their own, while finding it a little bit overwhelming or intimidating!

Demon's Souls

Grandfather of the Souls series. These tips are both for original PS3 release as well as the new PS5 remake.

General tips:

World Tendency:
One of the more unique mechanics of the game is World Tendency. It can shift towards white or black depending on your actions. Good deeds such banishing black phantoms, killing bosses etc. shift current level's tendency towards white while killing NPCs and so on towards black. White tendency opens up previously unaccessable areas and generaly makes your journey easier. Black makes enemies stronger, adds additional enemies and so on. Here's the catch: dying as a human form also shifts tendency towards black and this can be a huge problem if you're already struggling with the game. The trick is to first get the Cling Ring from level 1-1. Normally when you die and turn into a soul form, your max health is halved. This ring only makes it dropby 25%. Additional bonus to being in soul form is that your footsteps are more silent, reducing enemy aggro range. After getting the ring and returning to Nexus, climb stairs up high, jump down and die. That way you can start the next level in soul form and don't have to worry about shifting tendency towards black by dying.

Beginning:

When you begin you get to choose your class and make your own character. If you prefer melee, the Knight might be the best for it's sturdy armor, shield etc. Royal is also really good, if you wish to go with magic. Which especially in this game is kinda overpowered and trivializes many fights. You'll then go through a tutorial level, not much to mention there. After that you'll be able to choose your own path. The recommended path for beginners starts something like 1-1, 1-2, 2-1, 3-1. That should get you going.

Misc:

  • Thief Ring can be found from level 1-1. It greatly reduces enemy aggro range and makes many areas (and even bosses) a lot easier. Use it with Cling Ring, stay in soul form and further lower the sound of your steps.
  • It's not a bad idea to grab a bow, upgrade it and use your spare souls on arrows. If you actually get really stuck, quite many of the bosses can be cheesed with bow.
  • Struggling? Magic Build is your answer. Magic is pretty overpowered in many of the Souls games, but here it is even more so.
  • You can find Dragon Longsword from level 2-2. One of the best weapons in the game.

Dark Souls

Demon's Souls was maybe the game that started it all, but this is the game that made the genre widely known.

General tips:

Souls and Dying:
You will die a lot. But don't let that discourage you. It is part of the learning experience. In Dark Souls when you kill something you get souls and when you die, you turn into a hollow and drop your souls. You get only one chance to get back to the point of your death and collect them. And you definetly want to, souls are the game's form of currency, used to level up your character, shop items etc. Also when you die, you become hollow. When in hollow form, you are unable to summon help. On the other hand, you also cannot be invaded by hostile NPCs or players.

Bonfires:
Bonfires are your friend. They heal you up, you can use them to level up, they refill your healing Estus Flasks and so on. But keep in mind, they also respawn every enemy. Humanity:
The number top left represents how much liquid humanities you have. It can be increased by using consumable called Humanity, or killing certain enemies and bosses. Using humanity heals great deal of health. It is also used at bonfires to restore your human form if hollowed. It can also be used to kindle bonfires. Kindling a bonfire increases the amount of Estus Flasks granted to you when resting there.

Beginning:

Choosing a character class is totally up to you. You will be able to change your build by leveling up later. Starting gift is also up to you. (Just... don't take binoculars.) Master Key is a double edged sword. It allows you to access certain later areas and good loot very early on. It also lets you skip certain hard areas. However as a beginner it might get you lost really bad.

Misc:

  • If you decide to go early through the Graveyard to the Catacombs in hopes of good loot, fine, but whatever you do, DO NOT take any bonfires besides the first one there. The way out is really hard, almost to the point where it's almost easier to restart the whole game.
  • Keep your equipment load low. The lower it is, the faster your roll is, fastest being when load is under 25%. But under 50% is already really good. While armor do offer protection, it is much less so than in many other games.
  • Use your souls. Idea of saving the currency for some big purchase may be tempting, but the risk of dying and losing all is ever constant. So if you have enough to level up or upgrade your weapon - do it. Not quite? Spend it on arrows, healing items etc. Stronger enemies eventually will yield more souls and you will get to buy what you want.

Dark Souls 2

The dark sheep of the family. The only one not directed by the father of the series, Hidetaka Miyazaki. Lot has changed since the first game. While no doubt flawed, this game brought new great elements (such as powerstancing weapons) to the series and the PVP is considered to be superior compared to other games by many.

General tips:

Souls:
Killing enemies yield souls (currency used for level upping, shopping etc.). However when you die you become hollow and drop the souls. You get only one chance to get back to that point and collect them or they'll be lost forever. Also your max health drops every time you die. When hollow you are unable to summon NPCs or other players for help. Unlike in the first game, this time it won't prevent others from incading you!

Bonfires:
Bonfires are your friend. They heal you up, you can use them to level up, they refill your healing Estus Flasks and so on. But keep in mind, they also respawn every enemy.

Beginning:

Once you complete the tutorial area (and may have noticed you don't need to fight every enemy) you arrive in Majula. This is the central hub of the game and a rare occasion of safety. Enjoy it. From here on you have pretty much two viable routes to progress: Forest of the Fallen Giants and Heide's Tower of Flame. I'd recommend starting with the former.

Misc:

  • In central hub Majula, don't join the Company of Champions covenant. It'll make enemies stronger and game generally harder.
  • In bottom of the Forest of the Fallen Giants, there is a cave with fire salamander. From a left side room in the cave you can find a Fire Longsword. It's a great early game weapon which will carry you far.
  • Return Estus Flask Shards to Emerald Herald in Majula and burn Sublime Bone Dusts at the bonfires. Both improve your healing Estus Flask, former the amount you carry, latter the healing potency.
  • In Forest of the Fallen Giants, near the first bonfire, you can find Merchant Hag Melentia. She sells you Lenigrast's Key, which opens the door for the Blacksmith in Majula. Blacksmith sells items and repairs broken equipment, but more importantly he upgrades your weapons and gear.
  • Always exhaust dialogue to the point of repeating themselves when talking to NPCs. They sometimes give you handy items, or may start a side quest.
  • From the very beginning focus on leveling up your ADP until your Agility is somewhere around 100 points. This makes healing animation slightly faster, but what you really want - and the game doesn't tell - is the invicibility frames (or i-frames) it provides. If you plan to dodge attacks instead of blocking, this makes your life so much easier. Of course you want to level up other attributes as well, so don't try to level it all the way up straight away.
  • There are many items (ex. Soul of a Hero, Soul of a Lost Undead etc.) which provide you with extra souls. Don't use them until you need them. You don't want to risk dying and losing them. Bosses also give you special kind of souls. You can use them, but it's best to save them. They can be later used for crafting special kind of items.
  • Keep equipment load low (unless you're going for tank build) for faster roll. Armor doesn't make THAT big of a difference anyway. On the same note, while leveling up attributes that scale with weapons you use is important, even more so you should focus on upgrading your weapons at the Blacksmith.

Dark Souls 3

With it's Bloodborne inspired fast-paced action, aesthetics and references to the first Dark Souls, many consider this to be the absolute best of the series.

General tips:

-

Beginning:

Starting Class:
Like always the character class is up to you. Knight is generally a good choice because of high-hitting weapon, good armor and 100% physical blocking shield. However, Mercenary is intriguing choice due to one small little thing: Sellsword Twinblades as starting weapon. It's one of the most overpowered PvE weapons in the game. You can also acquire it later though.

Now, you'll face your mandatory first boss very early on and before a chance to level up. This is the first wall for many new players and can be frustrating. This boss is all about teaching you the essential mechanics of the combat: parry, blocking, dodging. Couple things to note: you can start dealing damage to him even before his health bar is visible. Keeping your shield up and strafing around him to your right makes most of his attacks miss. Also viable tactic is to keep your distance. Observe and learn his attacks. There's guaranteed to be at least some that are easily read and dodged. Focus on dodging those and use the following opening to attack. Rinse and repeat. On his seconds phase (yes, you read right) he becomes faster and more intimidating but the same tactics still work.

Bloodborne

It's time for some Victorian Gothic Lovecraftian horror! Dark Souls taught players to be calculative, shield up and take encounters calm and easy. Along came Bloodborne and threw all that out of the window. Now we get fast and aggressive.

General tips:

The risk-reward in combat is a very delicately balanced mechanic. When you take damage, you have brief moment to attack the enemy. Each succesful hit regains little bit of your lost health back! So it pays to be aggressive and fast, but getting too greedy here may also get you killed fast.

Beginning:

You'll quickly notice that you'll start without a weapon. Once you die (or reach the first Lamp) you'll be taken to the Hunter's Dream - the central hub of the game - where you get to choose your weapon. If you want the most overpowered, good all around, high damage-per-second weapon, the Saw Cleaver is the way to go.

Misc:

  • Don't treat your gun as a way to do damage: it's purpose is to parry enemy attacks and open them for a Visceral Attack.
  • Keep your eyes open for shortcuts, gates and doors you can open.
  • There is one shield in the game - and it's completely useless. Don't bother.
  • Each weapon is a trick weapon with two different modes you can switch between by pressing L1. It changes attacks, range and speed radically. Better learn to use both. They can even be chained as a combo.
  • To unlock leveling up, you need Insight level of at least 1. Easiest way to get there, is to find an optional first level boss, Cleric Beast, and confront it. You don't need to win, just trigger the encounter.

Sekiro

Welcome to the Sengoku Period! After 5 games, From Software decided to radically change the formula and freshen up the series.

General tips:

This game differs a lot from the traditional Souls formula. For some it is the absolute hardest, for some easiest. This time farming souls and leveling up isn't an option. You can farm and learn new skills, but attack power is only increased by beating bosses, while increasing max health is increased by collecting Prayer Beads (it takes 4 to upgrade). You get Prayer Beads mostly by - you guessed it - defeating difficult mini bosses.

Movement and Combat:
The movement and approach to areas is now much more free compared to the previous titles and sneaking is totally a viable option. Since upgrading your stats and powering through enemies is not an option this time, you are forced to learn the actual combat. It revolves around parrying. Attacking and parrying enemy attacks raises their posture gauge and eventually opens them for a kill blow. When enemy is blocking and eventually parries your attack, you can hear very specific different kind of cling sound. Get ready now to deflect one of their attacks. Combat in Sekiro is almost like a dance, constantly going back and forth.

Prosthetic Tools:
This time instead of different kind of weapons you are stuck with your trusted katana. However, there are many Prosthetic Tools you can find and upgrade in different kind of ways. These Tools include such as Shurikens, Spear, Firecrackers etc. These bring more tactical element to the encounters, especially with the bosses. Different kind of enemies have different weaknesses, for example: enemies with red eyes fear fire while Firecrackers are excellent against beasts.

Misc:

  • Most bosses and minibosses have two health bars. Many times taking the sneaky approach may allow you to start the fight with a death blow, making the fight easier.
  • Do NOT spam deflect. Window for parrying is fairly generous 30 frames (0.5 seconds). However, if you spam it, each unsuccesful attempt narrows the window.
  • This time once you fie you can resurrect yourself once (later multiple times.) Use this as a tactical advance, since it usually de-aggros enemies giving you back the element of surprise. It also can be used to escape. Be careful though, as enemies' posture gauge also lowers the longer you wait.
  • Red Kanji above enemy's head indicates an unblockable attack is incoming. Get ready to dodge thrust attacks, or jump over and stomp on their head if they sweep.
  • Ability you should prioritize buying as soon as possible: Mikiri Counter. It allows you to deflect Red Kanji thrust attacks while dealing huge amount of posture damage.
  • Other important abilities:
    Breath of Life: Light - recovers healt upon deathblows, Grappling Hook Attack, Ascending Carp and Descending Carp - both increase posture damage dealt.

Elden Ring

Miyazaki himself combined forces with author George R.R. Martin and brings us now the biggest adventure yet, in the form of an open-world adventure!

General tips:

Sites of Grace:
Sites of Grace are this games form of checkpoint. At them you can trade your runes (got from enemies etc.) into levels and do other kinds of useful activities. Some of them also emit a dim string of light. They usually lead to places affiliated with main quests. There is also Stakes of Marika close to bosses and important locations. When dying, the game present you with an option to continue from there, or respawn at the last Site of Grace visited.

Misc:

  • To fill in the missing pieces of your map, you need to find the actual map of each corresponding area. They are found from small obelisk kind of structures, which are actually drawn in the map.
  • When you reach the first merchant, it is highly recommended to prioritize buying the crafting kit immediately. Do not underestimate the value of being able to craft poison cures, arrows etc. on the go.
  • Just because a boss resides the starting area, it doesn't mean they're supposed be tackled early on.
  • Do not spam dodge roll, be meticulous. Enemies in this game have cleverly delayed tracking attacks and they will catch and punish you.
  • One of the new mechanics is guard counters (pressin heavy attack right adter blocking an attack), they are really useful for fast countering, dealing great damage and possibly stunning the enemy.
  • To the East from the starting location is the Agheel Lake. From the Northern part of it you can find Limgrave Tunnels, which offers a lot of early game Smithing Stones for upgrading weapon.
  • It's not bad idea to make your build so you are prepared to use variety of weapons (dex, str etc.), because this time - more than ever before - you will be facing great variety of enemies with different weaknesses. So it's a good idea to have some sort of slashing, long-range and striking weapon at hand at all the times.
  • If you are up for a challenge, you can find and enter Forlorn Hound Evergaol, South from the Agheel Lake. You'll encounter a strong enemy, but if you manage to beat him, you'll be rewarded with a Bloodhound's Fang, a special Str/Dex scaling weapon, which also causes a bleed build-up. It will melt through many of the early game enemies and is still viable later with upgrading.
  • You get your trustworthy horse early on. Besides faster travel between locations, mounted combat (hit-and-run) can trivialize many of the overworld mini bosses.
  • Once you have explored the starting area (which will take hours) and, after your first real boss, you arrive at the gates of the Stormveil Castle. Especially if you've been struggling, this might be a good moment to take a little bit bigger detour and travel to the Southern part of the map. There lies Weeping Peninsula. Lots of good gear and dungeons to clear.
  • If you happen to stumble upon some Rune Arcs, save them for later. They are one time use, last only until you die, and they require you to first find something to really benefit you.
